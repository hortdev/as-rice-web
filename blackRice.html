Black Rice

Black rice, often called "forbidden rice," is a nutrient-rich whole grain known for its deep purple-black color and slightly nutty flavor. This ancient grain originated in Asia and was historically reserved for royalty, symbolizing wealth and health. Packed with antioxidants, particularly anthocyanins (the same pigments found in blueberries), black rice is considered a superfood.

Unlike white rice, black rice retains its bran layer, making it high in fiber, vitamins, and minerals. It also has a low glycemic index, making it a healthier choice for maintaining steady energy levels. When cooked, black rice transforms into a stunning dark purple hue, adding visual appeal to any dish.
