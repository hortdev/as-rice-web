Arborio Rice

Arborio rice is a short-grain variety named after the town of Arborio in Italy's Po Valley. Known for its high starch content, this rice becomes creamy when cooked, making it the perfect choice for dishes like risotto. Its grains are plump, oval-shaped, and slightly pearly in color, with a mild, nutty flavor that absorbs the taste of other ingredients beautifully. Arborio rice is also versatile, used not only in risottos but also in soups, rice puddings, and Italian fried rice balls known as arancini.

What sets Arborio rice apart is its ability to achieve a creamy texture without adding cream, thanks to the starch released during cooking. To prepare it, you don't rinse the rice; instead, it's cooked slowly with broth and frequent stirring to draw out its rich, velvety texture. This Italian staple has become a favorite worldwide for creating hearty, flavorful dishes.
