Calrose Rice

Calrose rice is a medium-grain rice variety that originated in California and is now widely recognized for its versatility in cooking. Known for its soft texture and slightly sticky consistency when cooked, Calrose rice is a favorite in dishes that require rice to hold together, such as sushi, rice bowls, and casseroles. Its mild flavor easily absorbs seasonings and complements a variety of cuisines, from Asian to Mediterranean.

Introduced in the 1940s, Calrose rice has become a staple in American households and is now grown in other regions worldwide. Its adaptable nature makes it ideal for both everyday meals and specialty recipes. Whether steamed, fried, or baked, Calrose rice delivers a satisfying, hearty base for countless dishes, making it a kitchen essential.
